Rules

  • Movement rules of standart chess apply.
  • Kings may not move into check, but may stay in check if they have a way out of it.
  • Players move at the same time. If your move would be illegal after your opponent's move, it is not played.
  • Should two pieces land on the same empty square, they both are removed except for kings.
  • Should two pieces land on the same occupied space, the piece of the colour of the occupant lands and stays there alone. Both other pieces are removed.
  • A move capturing a piece in one's home ranks (1 and 2 for White, 7 and 8 for Black) has priority, as to prevent rampages
  • Your opponent wins if your king gets captured but not theirs (capture) or if you have no way of escaping check on the next turn (checkmate).
  • The game ends in a draw under standart chess circumstances and when both kings are captured or checkmated on the same turn. Lack of mating material is now only the case when only one bishop or knight remains.
